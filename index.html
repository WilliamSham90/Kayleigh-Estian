Kayleigh

Kayleigh grew up with a disposable camera glued to her hands, turning ordinary Sunday afternoons into golden-hour adventures. By twenty, she was shooting weddings across the Eastern Cape, chasing light into abandoned farmhouses, and developing film in a bathroom she converted into a darkroom.

Her photographs don't just document moments — they breathe. Every frame is a love letter to whatever stood still long enough to be seen. It was at one of those weddings that she first spotted a certain dark-haired guitarist who most definitely did not stand still.

Estian

Estian
The Man.
The Myth.
The Riff.

Estian picked up his first guitar at eleven, immediately broke a string, and considered it a sign from the music gods that he was meant for something heavier. By seventeen he was fronting No Closure — three sweaty teenagers in a garage, playing riffs loud enough to rattle the neighbour's security gate.

Two decades, four albums, and countless broken eardrums later, Estian still plays every show like it's the last one on earth. Off stage, he's surprisingly gentle — a man who cries at nature documentaries, bakes sourdough obsessively, and fell completely apart the moment a certain photographer pointed a camera at him from the back of the crowd.
